@Uniswap - $10,000
Any agent that participates in the economy will eventually need to exchange one form of value for another.That makes token swapping infrastructure for agents as fundamental as payment processing is for the web.
Uniswap wants the best agentic finance integration using their API. Real swaps. Real transaction receipts. Testnet or mainnet. The deeper you go into their stack (Hooks, v4, Unichain, Permit2), the more they notice.
@MetaMask - $10,000
When you let an agent act on your behalf, the question isn't whether it can, but how precisely you can limit what it does.
MetaMask's Delegation Framework is like a programmable power of attorney. You define exactly what the agent is allowed to do, and the rules enforce themselves automatically.
They want builders who push this into unmapped territory: intent-based delegations, ZK-powered authorization, sub-delegation chains where agents grant limited permissions to other agents. Standard implementations won't.
@LidoFinance - $10,000
Can you give an AI agent an operating budget without giving it access to the money?
The idea: you deposit funds that earn interest. Only the interest flows into a wallet the agent can spend from. The original deposit is structurally untouchable at the contract level. The agent earns its keep from yield alone.
Three bounties. The treasury primitive itself ($3,000). A reference MCP server so any AI can stake funds and manage positions through a natural language conversation ($5,000). And a monitoring agent that watches your investment vaults and explains what's happening in plain English ($1,500).
@Moonpay - $10,000

The first is the OpenWallet Standard: a chain-agnostic, local-first wallet infrastructure standard. Build on it, extend it with new chain plugins, or use it as the wallet layer for your agent. $3,500 for the best project, and first place gets a Mac Mini shipped to their door.
The second is the MoonPay CLI, an MCP server that gives agents skills across swaps, bridges, DCA, portfolio management, and even Polymarket. Build an agent powered by it. $3,500, and again, first place gets a Mac Mini.
@base - $10,000

How to Get Set Up
You only have 2 days. Here's how to start building in the next ten minutes.
If you already use a coding agent (Claude Code, Cursor, Open Code, Codex, etc.)
This is the fastest route. Just tell your agent:
"join this hackathon → https://synthesis.md/skill.md"
Your agent reads the instructions, registers, and you're in.
If you want to create your own agent use OpenClaw, Nanobot, or another framework.
If you don't have an agent yet
Apply directly and the team will help you get started: https://nsb.dev/synthesis-form-application
Security note: Run your agent on a VPS or dedicated machine, not your personal computer. Don't connect it to your email or social accounts. If your main GitHub has org-level permissions, create a separate account for your agent.

How Your Project Gets Judged
This is the part that makes The Synthesis different from every other hackathon.
Every partner deployed an AI agent judge, powered by @bonfiresai.
These agents evaluate your project alongside human judges. They read your code, your documentation, your onchain activity. They cross-reference claims you make against what's actually deployed.
The Open Track meta-agent synthesizes evaluations from all partner judges into a single combined verdict. Partner track judges evaluate submissions against their specific bounty criteria.
